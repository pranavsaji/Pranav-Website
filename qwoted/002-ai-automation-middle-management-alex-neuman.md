## What the Reporter Is Looking For

An expert who can speak to the hidden costs of removing middle management through AI automation. The show references Dr. McWealth's view that middle managers buffer teams from executive pressure, and that AI flattens org charts without absorbing the stress load those managers carried. Looking for insights on psychological safety, burnout, turnover, and organizational change when AI takes over management layers.

## Pitch

Hi Alex,

I'm Pranav Saji, Head of AI Security at Symosis Security and a Machine Learning Consultant at LinkedIn. I build the AI automation systems that replace these workflows, and I have led engineering teams through the organizational changes that follow.

That dual perspective is what I think would add something different to this conversation. I have shipped production AI agents that eliminated significant manual work across enterprise environments, and I have also scaled teams of 15 or more people through exactly the kind of restructuring your show is examining. I have watched what happens when a layer of coordination disappears: who absorbs the context that layer was holding, how communication breaks down in ways nobody planned for, and why the stress does not go away just because the org chart got flatter.

The point that resonates most with me from what you shared: AI can automate a task. It cannot automate the judgment calls that happen between the task and the outcome. Middle managers were making hundreds of those invisible decisions every day. When that layer is removed, those decisions do not disappear. They either land on someone else or they go unmade.

I am happy to join in English and can bring specific examples from building and deploying these systems in real enterprise environments.

And at a healthtech company, I designed a HIPAA-compliant Trusted Research Environment that handled sensitive hospital documents across multiple countries, with full access controls, audit trails, and chain-of-custody requirements.

A few concrete things I can bring to your story:

First, the trustworthiness question is almost never about the AI model itself. It is about what the model can see, what it cannot see, and whether the system around it maintains provenance. An AI that summarizes a document correctly 99 percent of the time will still cause compliance failures if it cannot tell you which version of the document it read.

Second, the governance frameworks that actually hold in regulated environments are not AI-specific. They are extensions of existing data governance: access control at the document level, immutable audit logs of what the AI read and when, and clear liability boundaries on who approved the AI output before it was acted on.

Third, the biggest gap I see across organizations is the absence of a human-in-the-loop requirement for high-stakes document decisions. Teams deploy AI for document review and then quietly remove the human review step because the AI seems accurate. That is where liability exposure compounds fast.
